Aviation Safety Risk Analysis

NTSB Historical Accident Data (1962–2023) · Phase 1 Data Science Project

PythonPandasMatplotlibSeabornEDAData CleaningBusiness Intelligence
+

Business Problem

A company looking to diversify its portfolio by entering the commercial aviation sector lacked domain expertise on the potential risks associated with different aircraft models and manufacturers.

The goal was to identify the lowest-risk aircraft for purchase by conducting a data-driven analysis of historical NTSB aviation accident data, balancing passenger safety with asset protection.

Data Preparation

The raw NTSB dataset required significant cleaning before analysis:

  • Removed write-offs — excluded aircraft that were completely destroyed, focusing only on survivable incidents
  • Excluded private operators — analysis focused solely on commercial/professional flights
  • Removed incomplete records — dropped rows with missing registrations, dates, or fatality data
  • Created two subsets — Substantial Damage (safety analysis) and Minor/No Damage (resilience analysis)
  • Extracted manufacturer from aircraft type string using Python string parsing

Key Findings

  • Cessna 208B Grand Caravan had the highest number of substantial-damage accidents with zero fatalities — top survivability leader with 55+ survived incidents
  • Boeing 737-8AS (WL) led operational resilience with 17+ minor/no-damage incidents, demonstrating exceptional structural durability
  • Embraer emerged as the safest manufacturer — highest number of incidents survived with zero deaths, outperforming Shorts, Raytheon, McDonnell, and Lockheed
  • Risk trend analysis (last 10 years) showed the number of substantial-damage commercial incidents was declining over time — indicating an improving safety environment
  • Airbus A330-243, A320-232, and Embraer ERJ-175LR also ranked highly for operational resilience

Recommendations

Rec 1 — Prioritise High Survivability Models

Select the Cessna 208B Grand Caravan as a primary acquisition. Its proven track record of zero fatalities across 55+ substantial-damage events minimises liability and reputational risk.

Rec 2 — Invest in Operationally Resilient Aircraft

Boeing 737-8AS (WL), Airbus A330-243, and A320-232 show highest minor-damage counts, indicating these aircraft are physically robust enough to withstand incidents without costly total-loss events.

Rec 3 — Partner with Proven Manufacturers

Limit initial fleet acquisitions to Embraer, Shorts, and Raytheon — manufacturers with the lowest average fatality rates per incident. Aligning with safety-focused manufacturers reduces systemic fleet risk.

Box Office Intelligence — Movie Studio EDA

5 Datasets · 3,359+ Films · 2010–2018 · Group 1, Moringa School

PythonPandasSeabornSciPyMulti-Dataset MergeROI AnalysisEDA
+

Business Problem

A company planning to launch a new movie studio had no experience in film production and no clear strategy for selecting which types of movies to produce. Given high production costs and uncertain returns, data-driven greenlight decisions were critical.

The analysis aimed to identify which genres, budget tiers, and quality levels deliver the best financial returns across 3,359+ films released 2010–2018.

Data Sources & Cleaning

SourceFilmsKey Issue Fixed
Box Office Mojo3,359Mixed string/float foreign_gross
The Numbers5,415Currency strings ($1,234,567) parsed
Rotten Tomatoes Info1,560Pipe-delimited genres exploded
Rotten Tomatoes Reviews54,432 reviews → 1,135 films scoredAggregated to fresh_pct per film

Key Findings

  • Sci-Fi & Fantasy averaged $81M per film — #1 earning genre driven by mega-hits with strong median performance
  • Action & Adventure offered the best volume-performance balance at $62M avg across 366 films
  • Animation had the lowest failure rate — nearly zero wide-release animated films lost money
  • Films budgeted $100M–$200M achieved a 93.1% profit rate with a 2.79× median ROI — the clear sweet spot
  • Certified Fresh films (RT ≥ 91%) earned +30% more than Rotten films ($47M vs $36M avg)
  • PG-13 rating maximised audience breadth at $57M avg vs $23M for R-rated films
  • Comedy & Drama dominated volume but averaged only $42M and $28M — inefficient for a new studio
  • Horror at micro-budget ($1–5M) offered exceptional ROI for cashflow diversification

Strategic Recommendations

Rec 1 — Lead with Sci-Fi, Action & Animation

These three genres average $56M–$81M per film. Target PG-13 for maximum audience breadth ($57M avg). Avoid Drama ($28M) and Documentary ($12M) as lead genres — too low-margin for a new studio. Use Horror micro-budget ($1–5M) as a secondary cash-flow play.

Rec 2 — Budget $100M–$200M per Tent-Pole

This tier achieves a 93.1% profit rate with 2.79× median ROI — meaning a $150M film earns ~$418M worldwide at median. Avoid the $10M–$50M range (only 66% profit rate, worst risk-adjusted tier). Scale to $200M+ as the studio matures.

Rec 3 — Invest in Quality — Target Certified Fresh

Certified Fresh films earn 30% more revenue. Hire directors with strong critical track records. Budget $2M–$5M for script development before greenlighting production. Release date flexibility is a competitive advantage — delay over cut quality.
